# Licensing Consultation Process

April 2025

Údarás Rialála Cearrbhachais na hÉireann  
The Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland

## Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland

Published on Monday the 7th day of April 2025

Submissions closed at 5.00pm on Monday the 5th day of May 2025

## Public Consultation Document

## 1. About us

We are the Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland. We were established on 5 March 2025. Our role is to regulate gambling activities in Ireland.

The law that established us, and sets out what we do, is the Gambling Regulation Act 2024. We refer to this law as **"the 2024 Act"** in this document.

Those who wish to provide gambling activities in Ireland will need a licence from us. We can issue different types of gambling licences for in person or remote gambling activities, including the following:

1. **Business to consumer gambling licences**  
   This type of licence applies to those providing betting, gaming or lottery activities to consumers.

2. **Business to business gambling licences**  
   This type of licence applies to those selling or supplying a gambling product or gambling related service.

3. **Licences for a charitable and philanthropic purpose**  
   This type of licence applies to those who wish to provide betting, gaming, or lottery activities for charitable and philanthropic purposes.

We intend to start our licensing function in a phased way, with different licence category types opening at different times.

Our current plan is to first open for Business to Consumer betting licence applications in December 2025 (both in person and remote). This will be followed by opening for gaming licence applications (remote only) towards the end of the first quarter of 2026. However, the order and timing of commencement maybe subject to change.

The license application process will take a number of months. Once an application is submitted it will be reviewed to ensure all necessary information and documentation has been submitted. We will then undertake various checks on the applicant and on the information and documentation submitted to verify the information and to ensure it is given due consideration when making the decision to grant or refuse the license application..

## 2. What is the purpose of this consultation?

We have the power to make regulations. Regulations are laws created by Ministers or public bodies under powers given to them by Acts of the Oireachtas.

The first set of regulations we plan to make will:

a. Set the application fee for applications for betting (in person and remote) and gaming (in person and remote) licences. This is provided for in section 38 of the 2024 Act.

The second set of regulations we plan to make will:

b. Set the duration for which a gambling licence remains in force. This is provided for under section 108 of the 2024 Act.

AND

c. Set out some additional conditions which will apply to gambling licences. This is provided for under section 129 of the 2024 Act.

We would like to hear the views of stakeholders, including operators and the public on the proposed regulations. To assist you in understanding the regulations, we have set out some further information below.

## 3. About the regulations

There are two sets of regulations relevant to this consultation.

### (a) Application Fee

The first set of regulations will set the application fee for betting (in person and remote) and gaming (in person and remote) licences.

Applicants can apply for more than one licence. The application fee is a non-refundable fee that must be paid in full at the time of applying.

We have carried out a detailed analysis of the market and the likely cost involved in assessing applications. We have also considered the approach taken to application fees in other jurisdictions. We are proposing to set application fees on a tiered fixed fee basis, by reference to turnover and licence type.

It is important to note that this fee specifically relates to the cost for submitting an application for a licence only. If a licence is granted, a licensee will in the future have to pay a separate charge to fund our operational costs. It is envisaged that this charge will be paid on an annual basis. In addition, a licensee will have to make an annual contribution to the Social Impact Fund. The purpose of the Social Impact Fund is to support initiatives to reduce and eliminate compulsive or excessive gambling. We will engage in a separate consultation process on the charge to fund our operational costs and the level of the annual contribution to be made to the Social Impact Fund. Please exclude reference to the charges for operational costs in your submissions, as it is envisaged that this will form part of a future consultation process

The draft regulations, which can be found at the following link, set out the application fees for each licence type. Proposed Fee Regulations

Some points to be aware of include:

1. For the purpose of these regulations, **turnover** should be understood as meaning –
   a. in the case of an applicant for a remote betting intermediary licence (see explanation at 5 below) the total amount paid to the applicant for providing the facility that enables a person to engage in betting.  
   b. in the case of an applicant for a betting and gaming licence (other than remote betting intermediary licence referred to at a above), the applicant's total income from betting and gaming activities less the total amount paid out in winnings for those activities.

This should be calculated on the basis of the applicant’s last financial year before the date of the application. Alternatively, if the applicant has not provided betting or gaming activities in the Republic of Ireland previously, the estimated turnover for the first 12 months following the grant of the licence.

2. Table A of Schedule 1 in the regulations deals with in-person betting licence applications, where betting activities are intended to be provided from a relevant premises. Reference to a relevant premises means on-course bookmaking.

3. Table B of Schedule 1 in the regulations deals with in-person betting licence applications where betting activities are intended to be provided from one or more premises other than a relevant premises. This means off course bookmaking, such as, for example, from a betting shop.

4. If the betting activity sought to be licensed is to be provided from both an on-course and off-course location, the applicant is required to pay the application fee in Table A of Schedule 1 in the regulations and the application fee in Table B of Schedule 1 in the regulations. The applicable fees should be added together.

5. Table D of Schedule 1 of the regulations sets out the applicable fee for remote betting intermediaries. A **remote betting intermediary** is a person or corporate entity (such as a company) that provides a facility to enable another person to engage in remote betting with others who are not the intermediary.

6. A gaming licence will enable the licensee to provide one or more games, either in person or remotely. The games that the licensee can provide will be clear from their licence. The Authority will soon prescribe a list of games which a licensee can provide. This may include games such as, slots, roulette, blackjack etc.

### (b) Licence duration and additional conditions

The second set of regulations will set the duration for which a gambling licence remains in force. They will also out set out some additional conditions which will apply to gambling licences. These regulations can be found here: Duration and additional proposed regulations

These regulations do two things, set out at i. and ii. below.

#### i. Licence duration

Section 108(4) of the 2024 Act allows us to set a period during which a Business to Consumer gambling licence remains in force.

Initially, we intend to set a period of 3 years duration for all Business to Consumer gambling licences.

This is consistent with, and supports, our phased approach to opening licensing. We want to be open for licensing for all Business to Consumer licence types before we have to consider applications for the renewal of licences.

#### ii. Additional conditions

There are a number of conditions that will apply to all licensees. These are set out in the 2024 Act.

In addition, we will make regulations setting out further conditions.

We intend to set additional conditions for remote Business to Consumer licensees. These conditions will apply to those providing remote gambling activities.

At the time when initial applicants are granted licenses, some standard licensing checks will not be carried out. These checks include:

- that where the software used or to be used has been manufactured or adapted by the proposed licensee, it meets particular standards in effect at that time.
- that where the software used or to be used has been manufactured or adapted by a third party, that the proposed licensee is or will be obtaining the software concerned from a Business-to-Business licensee.
- that where the proposed licensee has installed or will install, maintains or will maintain, and upgrades or will upgrade the software used or to be used, that such gambling related services meet standards in effect at that time.
- that where a third party has installed or will install, maintains or will maintain, and upgrades or will upgrade the software used or to be used, that such gambling related services meet standards in effect at that time.
- that software to be used by the licensee enables the licensee to view, in real time, the information contained in the National Gambling Exclusion Register. This register will contain information on people who have excluded themselves from participating in remote gambling.

The reason that these checks will not be carried out when initial applicants are granted licences is because, at that time, relevant standards under section 123 of the 2024 Act will not have been set by us and will not be in effect. These standards include standards relating to the software used for the operation of gambling. In addition, at that time, the National Gambling Exclusion Register will not be established in such a way that licensees will be able to access in real time information contained in that register.

These conditions will ensure that after standards take effect, and after the National Gambling Exclusion Register is established in the appropriate way, existing licensees will be required to provide the necessary information and documentation to allow us to carry out the necessary checks. These are checks that would ordinarily be carried out at the time of consideration of an application.

## 4. How to take part in this consultation

We would like your feedback on the proposed regulations. If you wish to take part in this consultation, please send your feedback using the following methods;

1. Email your submission to: consultations@grai.ie
2. Post your submission to: The Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland, Ballaugh House, 73 Mount Street Lower, Dublin, 2, D02 PX37

Please note –

- Submissions should be limited to a maximum of 1,000 words.
- Please use headings to make it clear what you are referring to, including which of the two sets of regulations you are referring to.
- Please address and confine your submissions to the content of these regulations.
- We may publish your response. If you do not wish to have your response published, or if you wish to have it published anonymously, please make this clear at the beginning of your response.
- In addition, we may publish a report of the consultation summarising the responses.
- Please note that responses to this consultation are subject to national legislation in respect of personal data, such as the Freedom of Information Act 2014 (FOI) and the Data Protection Act 2018 (which implemented the European Union, General Data Protection Regulation). We would like to draw your attention to our Privacy Notice that explains how and when we collect personal data, why we do so and how we treat this information
- We will be making further regulations in the future and, where appropriate, there will be a consultation in relation to these further regulations at that time.
