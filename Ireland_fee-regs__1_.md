# GAMBLING (LICENCE APPLICATION FEES) REGULATIONS 2025

**S.I. No. of 2025**

Údarás Rialála Cearrbhachais na hÉireann (The Gambling Regulatory Authority of Ireland), in exercise of the powers conferred on it by sections 9 and 38 of the Gambling Regulation Act 2024 (No. 35 of 2024) (as adapted by the Justice (Alteration of Name of Department and Title of Minister) Order 2025 (S.I. No. XXX of 2025)), hereby makes the following regulations:

### 1. Citation

These Regulations may be cited as the Gambling (Licence Application Fees) Regulations 2025.

### 2. Commencement

These Regulations come into operation on XXXX 2025.

### 3. Definition

**Act** means the Gambling Regulation Act 2024 (No. 35 of 2024).

### 4. Fees for applications for betting licences

#### 4. (1)

The fees payable on making an application for an in-person betting licence under Part 5 of the Act are as follows, as applicable:

- a. where the betting activity sought to be licensed is proposed to be provided from one or more than one relevant premises, the fee in column 2 of Table A in Schedule 1 corresponding to the turnover bracket in column 1 of the said table applicable to the last financial year of the applicant preceding the date of the application, or, where the applicant has not provided in-person betting activities in the State previously from one or more than one relevant premises, the estimated turnover from the provision of such activities for the first twelve months following the grant of the licence; and
- b. where the betting activity sought to be licensed is proposed to be provided from one or more than one premises in the State other than a relevant premises, the fee in column 2 of Table B in Schedule 1 corresponding to the turnover bracket in column 1 of the said table applicable to the last financial year of the applicant preceding the date of the application, or, where the applicant has not provided in-person betting activities in the State previously from one or more than one premises other than a relevant premises, the estimated turnover from the provision of such activities for the first twelve months following the grant of the licence, together with an additional fee of €1,200 for each premises in the State from where the betting activity sought to be licensed is proposed to be provided.

#### 4. (2)

The fee payable on making an application for a remote betting licence under Part 5 of the Act is the fee in column 2 of Table C in Schedule 1 corresponding to the turnover bracket in column 1 of the said table applicable to the last financial year of the applicant preceding the date of the application, or, where the applicant has not provided remote betting activities in the State previously, the estimated turnover from the provision of such activities for the first twelve months following the grant of the licence.

#### 4. (3)

The fee payable on making an application for a remote betting intermediary licence under Part 5 of the Act is the fee in column 2 of Table D in Schedule 1 corresponding to the turnover bracket in column 1 of the said table applicable to the last financial year of the applicant preceding the date of the application, or, where the applicant has not provided remote betting intermediary activities in the State previously, the estimated turnover from the provision of such activities for the first twelve months following the grant of the licence.

### 5. Fees for applications for gaming licences

#### 5. (1)

The fees payable on making an application for an in-person gaming licence under Part 5 of the Act are as follows:

- (a) the fee in column 2 of Table A in Schedule 2 corresponding to the turnover bracket in column 1 of the said table applicable to the last financial year of the applicant preceding the date of the application, or, where the applicant has not provided in-person gaming activities in the State previously, the estimated turnover from the provision of such activities for the first twelve months following the grant of the licence; and;
- (b) a fee of €1,200 for each premises in the State from where the game or games sought to be licensed are proposed to be provided.

#### 5. (2)

The fee payable on making an application for a remote gaming licence under Part 5 of the Act is the fee in column 2 of Table B in Schedule 2 corresponding to the turnover bracket in column 1 of the said table applicable to the last financial year of the applicant preceding the date of the application, or, where the applicant has not provided remote gaming activities in the State previously, the estimated turnover from the provision of such activities for the first twelve months following the grant of the licence.
